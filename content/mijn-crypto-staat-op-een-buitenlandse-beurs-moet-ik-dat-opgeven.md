<h2>Understanding Foreign Crypto Holdings</h2>
<p>As a Dutch taxpayer, holding cryptocurrency on a foreign exchange can raise questions about your tax obligations. The Netherlands treats crypto assets as part of Box 3, which covers savings and investments. Understanding whether you need to declare these holdings is crucial for staying compliant.</p>
<p>In 2026, the Dutch Box 3 tax rate is set at 36%, with a deemed return on crypto investments at 6.00%. Every taxpayer is entitled to a tax-free allowance of €59,357, or €118,714 for fiscal partners. These rates and allowances are essential when evaluating your crypto tax liabilities.</p>

<h2>When to Declare Foreign Crypto Assets</h2>
<p>Any cryptocurrency held on foreign exchanges must be declared if the total value of your assets exceeds the tax-free allowance. <p>The critical date for determining the value of your crypto assets is January 1st of the tax year. For example, for your 2026 tax return, you must calculate and report your crypto holdings as of January 1, 2026.</p>

<h3>Example Calculation</h3>
<p>Consider a scenario where you hold 3 Bitcoin on a foreign exchange. If the value of Bitcoin on January 1, 2026, is €40,000, your total crypto asset value is €120,000. If you have no fiscal partner, this surpasses the €59,357 tax-free allowance, requiring you to report it as part of your Box 3 income.</p>

<h2>Impact of DAC8 Reporting Rules</h2>
<p>Starting January 1, 2026, the EU's DAC8 reporting rules will enforce stricter regulations on crypto transactions. These rules mandate that foreign exchanges report crypto holdings of EU citizens, ensuring transparency and compliance. Hence, not declaring your crypto assets could lead to penalties, given the increased scrutiny.</p>
