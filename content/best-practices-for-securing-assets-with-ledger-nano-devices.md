Proper setup is the foundation of securing your assets. Follow these steps to ensure your <a href='https://ledger.com' target='_blank' rel='noopener nofollow sponsored'>Ledger Nano</a> device is correctly configured:

### Unboxing and Initial Configuration

1. **Purchase from a Reliable Source**: Only buy <a href='https://ledger.com' target='_blank' rel='noopener nofollow sponsored'>Ledger Nano</a> devices from the official Ledger website or verified retailers to avoid counterfeit products.
   
2. **Inspect the Packaging**: Ensure that the packaging is intact and shows no signs of tampering. Authentic Ledger devices come with a tamper-proof seal.

3. **Initial Device Setup**: Connect your <a href='https://ledger.com' target='_blank' rel='noopener nofollow sponsored'>Ledger Nano</a> to a computer using the provided USB cable. Follow the on-screen instructions to initialize the device. Choose a secure PIN code that is difficult to guess.

### Securely Storing Your Recovery Phrase

1. **Write Down Your Recovery Phrase**: During setup, you will receive a 24-word recovery phrase. Write it down on the recovery sheet provided, and never store it digitally.

2. **Keep Multiple Backups**: Store copies of your recovery phrase in different secure locations. Consider using a safe or deposit box for added security.

3. **Never Share Your Recovery Phrase**: Your recovery phrase is the key to accessing your funds. Never share it online or with anyone you do not trust completely.

## Advanced Security Measures

For those looking to enhance their security further, consider these advanced measures:

### Implementing Passphrase Protection

1. **Use a Passphrase**: Ledger devices allow you to add a passphrase to your recovery phrase, creating a hidden wallet. This feature adds another layer of security but requires careful management.

2. **Manage Passphrase Securely**: Ensure you remember your passphrase, as forgetting it can result in loss of access to your assets.

### Multisignature Wallets

1. **Set Up Multisig Wallets**: For higher security, use multisignature wallets that require multiple signatures to authorize transactions. This setup can protect against single-point failures.

2. **Diversify Signatories**: Choose trusted individuals or devices as signatories to reduce the risk of compromised access.
